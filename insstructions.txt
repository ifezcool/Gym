Fix 1 — Button spacing in ps_provider_layout
Find the html.Div containing provider-nav-view-btn and provider-nav-submit-btn. The view button currently has "marginRight": "10px" but the buttons are still cramped. Add "marginBottom": "8px" to each button's style, increase the marginRight on the view button to "15px", and add "display": "flex", "flexWrap": "wrap", and "gap": "10px" to the wrapping html.Div instead of relying on individual margins. Remove the marginRight from the individual button styles since the gap on the container handles spacing uniformly.

Fix 2 — Provider view starts blank instead of showing the table on load
The update_provider_content callback currently has prevent_initial_call=True and only fires when one of the two nav buttons is clicked. This means on first login, nothing renders until a button is pressed.
Change prevent_initial_call=True to prevent_initial_call=False on that callback. Then at the top of the callback function body, update the triggered ID logic: when ctx.triggered is empty OR when the triggered prop is something other than provider-nav-submit-btn (including the initial call with no trigger), default option to "view". This means the table and summary cards will render immediately when the provider portal loads after login, without requiring any button click.
